Ukrainian Summer school in Combinatorics 2025

Organisers: Johannes Carmesin, Oleg Pikhurko

September 15-19, 2025 at TU Freiberg (Germany)

Invited Speakers

  • Marthe Bonamy
  • Dan KráÍ
  • Volodymyr Nekrashevych
  • Danylo Radchenko

Synopsis

Our summer school is aimed at PhD students, postdocs, and excellent undergraduate students working in Combinatorics or related areas from across Europe. We have the additional goal to build a network for Ukrainian Scientists with connections to Combinatorics. The summer school is accompanied by a satelite workshop aimed at postdocs and more senior researchers. It is possible to attend any combination of these events. These events takes place at TU Freiberg.
TU Freiberg, founded in 1765, is a research university located in the Iron Mountains in Saxony (Germany). There are quite a few hiking opportunities nearby, for example in Sächsische Schweiz. Its Historic town centre has a variety of restaurants with traditional German/Czech food, such as Stadtwirtschaft (Einstein themed, traditional Czech food), Himmel und Hölle (if you are up for a surprise), Ratskeller (traditional German food), Kartoffelhaus (traditional German food, a few vegetarian options as well).

Overall structure. In the mornings, we start with overview lectures from the invited speakers. These will be followed by an example class where we apply the newly learned methods. In the afternoon we offer problem solving sessions. Each day there would be various options to choose from, and the idea is that participants would work on problems in Combinatorics in small groups. These problem solving sessions are fairly flexible and need not be tied to a particular lecture. In the strategy meeting on Thursdays evening, we will discuss possible joint future grant applications, and potential future meetings. If you are planning to come for the satelite workshop, please consider taking part in the strategy meeting as well if useful to you.

Ukrainian Prize in Combinatorics

At the satelite workshop, we will award the Ukrainian Prize for Combinatorics. It will be awarded to a researcher with connections to Ukraine who must not have obtained a PhD more than 7 years ago. The prize of 1000 Euros is funded by TU Freiberg and Suuuport. The selection committee consists of Marthe Bonamy, Johannes Carmesin, Dan KráÍ, Oleg Pikhurko, Volodymyr Nekrashevych and Danylo Radchenko. Please do consider to suggest candidates via email to Johannes Carmesin. To nominate a candidate, it suffices to write a short sentence, and add a link to their personal webpage or attach a CV. We may later reach out to aquire more information. You will receive a confirmation email that we received your nomination.

Registration

Please register as early as possible (to help us with the planning). Also there may be additional benefits. In order to register, please write an email to Mrs Löschner. Please consider registering early since in these circumstances we may be able to help with reduced prizes for accommodation, and some of the conference funding may also be awarded before the event. We offer online and/or remote participation, to sign up for this, please write an email to Mrs Löschner. It is possible to register for the summer school, the satelite workshop, or both. If you register for the satelite workshop, please state whether you would like to give a talk. We will confirm your registration within two working days. We still have free slots for postdocs or more senior researchers to offer problem solving workshops at the summer school. If you would be interested, please let us know.

Funding

There is no conference fee. We have a non-negligible amount of funding to support travel and accommodation. Our sponsors are TU Freiberg and Suuuport, and further funding may be available via DAAD. Priority will be given to Ukrainian participants.
